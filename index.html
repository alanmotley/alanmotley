Perspective

My work is driven by a simple belief: the future of AI is not just about capability. It is about structure, framing, governance, and whether people can actually trust what systems produce.

Why this matters

We are entering a period where AI systems increasingly shape what people experience as credible, useful, safe, and true. That means design choices, retrieval, ranking, tuning, and policy layers are governance decisions, not just product details.

What I bring

I connect enterprise systems thinking, narrative sensitivity, engineering, and consulting execution. That combination lets me see not just what a system can do, but how it behaves, how it is understood, and what trust it actually earns.

Now Building: Norynthe

Independent standards for trusted intelligence.

The idea Norynthe is an independent AI trust infrastructure company designed to evaluate how models construct answers, where they diverge, what they omit, how their framing shifts, and how trustworthy those outputs are under pressure.
Why it matters As AI becomes embedded into products, workflows, and public understanding, organizations need a clearer outside standard for comparison, trust, grounding, evidence strength, and framing stability.
